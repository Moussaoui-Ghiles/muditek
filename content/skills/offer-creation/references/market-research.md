# Live Market Research Workflow

Use live web research before constructing or adapting an offer. Do not rely only on stored knowledge for current market claims.

## Goals
Research must answer:
1. Is the pain active now?
2. What exact language do buyers use?
3. What offers already exist?
4. What pricing models and price bands exist?
5. What guarantees or risk reversals are common?
6. What objections repeat?
7. Where is the whitespace?

## Tools
- Use `web_search` for discovery
- Use `web_fetch` to read pages quickly
- Use `browser` only when page structure or dynamic content requires it

## Research waves

### Wave 1: demand and pain
Search for current pain, failed attempts, and urgency.

Useful patterns:
- `<ICP> struggling with <problem>`
- `<problem> is hard because`
- `how to fix <problem> reddit`
- `site:reddit.com <problem>`
- `site:quora.com <problem>`
- `alternatives to <current solution>`
- `best way to <desired outcome> 2026`

### Wave 2: competitors and offers
Map direct and adjacent offers.

Useful patterns:
- `<problem> service`
- `<problem> done-for-you`
- `<problem> coaching`
- `<category> pricing`
- `<competitor> pricing`
- `<competitor> guarantee`
- `<competitor> refund policy`

### Wave 3: pricing, guarantees, objections
Look for explicit pricing and real friction.

Useful patterns:
- `how much does <solution> cost`
- `is <solution> worth it`
- `<category> scam review`
- `<category> refund denied`
- `results not typical <category>`
- `before and after <solution>`

### Wave 4: whitespace
Find underserved segments and unmet needs.

Useful patterns:
- `<ICP> <problem> no time`
- `<ICP> <problem> no team`
- `I tried <solutions> and none worked`
- `why <common approach> fails`
- `<problem> for beginners`
- `<problem> for agencies`
- `<problem> for solo founders`

## Evidence to collect
For each useful signal, capture:
- claim type
- verbatim quote when possible
- source URL
- source type
- date
- region if relevant
- confidence
- note on why it matters

Suggested claim types:
- demand
- pain phrase
- competitor
- price
- guarantee
- objection
- whitespace

## Minimum evidence standards
Aim for:
- 20+ pain / objection verbatims across 3+ source types
- 8–12 direct competitors and several adjacent ones
- 10–15 price references when available
- 10+ guarantee examples when available
- 5+ repeated whitespace signals

If evidence is weak, say so explicitly.

## Synthesis rules
- Cluster pain phrases into themes
- Count which themes repeat most often
- Separate facts from inference
- Prefer recent sources
- Downgrade confidence when evidence conflicts or is sparse
- Do not invent pricing, guarantees, or demand signals
- Keep source diversity; do not let one platform dominate all conclusions

## Research output
Return:
1. Demand summary
2. Pain-language themes with quotes
3. Competitor landscape
4. Price bands
5. Guarantee patterns
6. Top objections
7. Whitespace opportunities
8. Offer implications for positioning, pricing, promise, and risk reversal
