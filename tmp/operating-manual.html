§ 03

Your weekly operating cadence

What you actually do, week after week, to keep the machines running. If you stick to this, the system runs itself.

Monday
Plan the week's content

Pick one deployable system you'll teach this week. Open Apple Notes or your vault. Sketch the outline in 30 minutes.

→ self/active-projects.md
Tuesday
Write the newsletter

Draft the issue. ~600–1200 words. One concrete win, one playbook, one CTA at the bottom.

→ /admin/newsletter → New issue
Wednesday
Send to HOT segment

Send to the 2,002 hot subs first. Watch open rate over 4 hours. If >25%, send to WARM at 6PM. If >15%, send to COLD next day.

→ /admin/newsletter/[id] → Send
Thursday
Post on LinkedIn

Repurpose the newsletter into a LinkedIn post. Add a keyword + lead-magnet hook. Activate the campaign.

→ /admin/campaigns → New campaign
Friday
Review the week

Check /admin: new subscribers, paid signups, calls booked, top-performing campaign. Note one thing to improve next week.

→ /admin (dashboard)
Sat–Sun
Off (or build paid kit)

Optional: write one paid playbook for /admin/content. Once 5 paid items exist, /buy stops selling vapor.

→ /admin/content → New (is_free=false)
§ 04

How to use each piece

Concrete steps for the four moves you'll make most often.

Send a newsletter

/admin/newsletter
  1. Click "New issue".
  2. Subject line first. Plain. Specific. No "🚀".
  3. Markdown body. H2 for sections. One CTA link at bottom.
  4. Pick audience: HOT / WARM / COLD / All / Custom topic.
  5. "Test send" to yourself first. Open it in Gmail mobile.
  6. "Send" → confirms count → fires through Resend.
  7. Check /admin/newsletter/[id] for opens/clicks 4 hrs later.

Add portal content

/admin/content
  1. Click "New item".
  2. Title, slug, category (playbook / guide / tool / blueprint).
  3. Description (1–2 sentences shown on the card).
  4. Download URL (PDF link or Notion link).
  5. Toggle is_free: on for free portal, off for paid kit.
  6. Toggle is_new for a "New" badge for 7 days.
  7. Save → appears in /portal immediately.

Launch a LinkedIn campaign

/admin/campaigns
  1. Click "New campaign".
  2. Title, keyword (e.g. "PLAYBOOK"), expiry date (2 weeks out).
  3. Lead magnet PDF URL.
  4. Save → get the /c/[id] landing URL.
  5. Post on LinkedIn: "Comment PLAYBOOK below to get the system →"
  6. Apify cron scrapes commenters daily.
  7. Verified emails get the magnet auto-sent.

Book-a-call lead

portal CTAs + offer pages
  1. Lead clicks "Book a call" on /pe-ops, /mudiagent, /revenue-leak-audit.
  2. External booking (Cal.com / Savvycal) handles the slot.
  3. Pre-call form captures: company, role, urgency, scope.
  4. Note added to clients/pipeline/[company]/.
  5. You take the call. Send proposal within 48 hrs.
  6. If won → company moves clients/<company>/ folder.
  7. Track in marketing/pipeline/pipeline.md.

§ 07

Decision shortcuts

The questions you'll ask yourself every week. Keep these answers in your head so you don't re-debate them.

Send to HOT only or to everyone?
HOT first. If open rate >25% in 4 hours, send to WARM. If WARM >15%, send to COLD next day. Never blast all 5,365 cold.
Newsletter topic this week?
Pick the most concrete win. Real client, real number, real playbook they can copy in 1 hour. Avoid theory weeks.
Should I add this to the free or paid kit?
Free if it teaches. Paid if it copy-pastes (templates, vault structures, prompts, scripts). Education = free, leverage = paid.
Do I take this client call?
Use decisions/decision-go-no-go-client.md. Tldr: revenue size, ops fit, urgency, payment terms. If 3 of 4 yes → take it.
Should I unsub a non-opener?
No. Resend handles reputation. Cold subs cost almost nothing. Re-engagement campaigns work better than purges.
Lead asks for a discount on /buy?
No discount. Offer a free 30-min audit instead. Discounts on $47 attract the wrong customer.
